How to Set Up Amazon Prime Video Login via Code on Your TV Screen

Amazon Prime Video is one of the world's most popular streaming services, offering thousands of movies, TV shows, and Amazon Originals. When setting it up on a smart TV, Roku, or Fire Stick, the easiest way to sign in is through a short on-screen activation code — no need to type your full email and password with a remote. This guide covers exactly how that TV code login works, plus how to fix the most common problems that come up along the way.

📺 How to Sign In to Amazon Prime Video Using a Code on Your TV

Amazon's "Register on the Web" method lets you link your TV app to your Amazon account by entering a short code at amazon.com/mytv — no typing your password with a remote. Here is exactly how it works.

  • Open the Prime Video app on your TV

    Launch the Prime Video app on your smart TV, Roku, Samsung TV, LG TV, or any other supported device. On the sign-in screen, choose the option that says "Register on the Amazon Website" or "Sign In & Start Watching."

  • Find the activation code displayed on your TV screen

    A short alphanumeric code — typically 6 characters like "ABCD12" — will appear on your TV screen. This is your one-time activation code. Keep this screen open and do not navigate away from it.

  • Go to amazon.com/mytv on a phone, tablet, or computer

    On any separate device, open a web browser and go to amazon.com/mytv. Sign in to your Amazon account if you are not already logged in. This must be the account that has an active Prime membership.

  • Enter the code shown on your TV

    Type the 6-character code from your TV screen into the field on amazon.com/mytv exactly as shown — codes are not case-sensitive. Click "Register" or "Continue" to link the device to your account.

  • Your TV will automatically sign in

    Within a few seconds, the Prime Video app on your TV will detect the registration and sign you in automatically. The code screen will disappear and you will land on the Prime Video home screen ready to watch.

Tip The activation code expires after about 10 minutes. If the timer runs out before you complete the steps, go back to the Prime Video app on your TV, exit to the sign-in screen, and select "Register on the Amazon Website" again to get a fresh code.

👤 How to Fix Amazon Prime Video Login Problems

If the code is not working, the page is showing an error, or you cannot get past the sign-in screen, work through these steps in order.

  • Make sure you are going to amazon.com/mytv — not a search result

    Type the URL directly into your browser address bar. Search engines sometimes surface unofficial or phishing pages. The correct address is exactly: amazon.com/mytv.

  • Confirm your Amazon account has an active Prime membership

    Prime Video is included with Amazon Prime. Go to amazon.com/myprime to check your membership status. If it has lapsed, renew it before trying to activate your TV again.

  • Get a new code if it expired or shows an error

    On your TV, press the Back button until you reach the Prime Video sign-in screen, then select "Register on the Amazon Website" again. Use the new code within 10 minutes.

  • Clear your browser's cache and cookies before entering the code

    An old browser session can cause amazon.com/mytv to fail silently. In Chrome: Settings → Privacy → Clear Browsing Data → check Cookies and Cached Images, then clear. Reload amazon.com/mytv and sign in again.

  • Try a different browser or device for the registration step

    If the page keeps spinning or showing an error, switch to Safari, Firefox, or Edge — or use a phone instead of a computer. Sometimes browser extensions block the registration page from completing.

Tip If you have multiple Amazon accounts (personal and work, for example), make sure you are signed in to the correct one at amazon.com/mytv — the account that owns your Prime membership. Mismatched accounts are the single most common cause of activation failures.

▶ How to Set Up Amazon Prime Video on Roku

Prime Video comes pre-installed on most Roku devices, but here is how to add it, update it, and complete the TV code login if you have never set it up.

  • Find Prime Video on the Roku home screen

    Press the Home button on your Roku remote. Scroll through your channel list to find "Prime Video." If it is not listed, press the Star (*) button and select "Add Channel" to install it from the Roku Channel Store.

  • Update Roku software if Prime Video fails to load

    Go to Settings → System → System Update → Check Now. Install any available updates and restart your Roku, then try opening Prime Video again.

  • Select "Sign In" and choose "Register on the Amazon Website"

    Launch the Prime Video channel. On the welcome screen select "Sign In," then choose the option to register via the website. An activation code will appear on your TV screen.

  • Go to amazon.com/mytv and enter the code

    On a phone or computer, visit amazon.com/mytv, sign in with your Prime account, and type the code from your Roku screen. Your Roku will sign in automatically within seconds.

  • Fix crashes by removing and re-adding the channel

    If Prime Video crashes or freezes on Roku, go to the Prime Video tile on the home screen, press Star (*), then select "Remove Channel." Restart your Roku, then add Prime Video back from the Channel Store.

Tip If Prime Video shows an error code like 5004 or 7017 on Roku, the issue is usually a network problem. Restart your router and modem, then go to Settings → Network → Check Connection on your Roku to verify it is online before opening Prime Video.

🔥 How to Set Up Amazon Prime Video on Fire Stick

Prime Video is built into every Amazon Fire TV Stick and Fire TV Cube, and typically signs you in automatically using your Amazon account. Here is how to set it up from scratch or fix a sign-in problem.

  • Check if Prime Video is already signed in

    Amazon Fire Stick registers itself to your Amazon account during initial setup. Open Prime Video from the home screen — if you can browse content, you are already signed in and ready to watch.

  • Switch accounts using the TV code method

    If your Fire Stick is linked to the wrong Amazon account, open Prime Video, go to Settings (the gear icon) → "Sign In & Start Watching." Choose "Register on the Amazon Website" to get a code, then go to amazon.com/mytv on another device to link the correct account.

  • Update Fire OS if Prime Video shows errors or crashes

    Go to Settings → My Fire TV → About → Check for System Updates. Install any pending updates, then reopen Prime Video. Outdated Fire OS is a common cause of Error 1060 and similar codes.

  • Clear the Prime Video app cache

    Settings → Applications → Manage Installed Applications → Prime Video → Clear Cache. If the issue persists, tap "Clear Data" — this signs you out of the app, so you will need to sign back in using the amazon.com/mytv code method.

Tip If you gave your Fire Stick to a family member or sold it, the previous owner's Amazon account may still be linked. Go to Settings → My Fire TV → Reset to Factory Defaults, then set it up fresh with the new account and use amazon.com/mytv to activate Prime Video cleanly.

📶 How to Fix Amazon Prime Video Buffering & Playback Issues

If Prime Video keeps buffering, showing pixelated video, or stopping mid-stream, the problem is almost always network speed or device memory. Work through these steps to resolve it.

  • Check your internet speed requirements

    Prime Video needs at least 1 Mbps for SD, 5 Mbps for HD, and 25 Mbps for 4K Ultra HD. Run a speed test at fast.com on the same Wi-Fi network your TV or streaming device uses. If speeds are low, restart your router by unplugging it for 30 seconds.

  • Move your router closer or use a wired connection

    Wi-Fi signal drops significantly through walls. If possible, plug your streaming device directly into your router using an Ethernet cable or a Powerline adapter. A wired connection eliminates the majority of buffering issues.

  • Lower the streaming quality inside Prime Video

    Open Prime Video → Settings (gear icon) → Stream & Download → Streaming Quality. Change from "Best" to "Good" or "Data Saver." This reduces the bandwidth required and stops buffering on slower connections.

  • Restart your device with a full power cycle

    Fully power off your TV, Roku, or Fire Stick — unplug it from the wall for 30 seconds rather than just using the remote's sleep function. A cold restart clears memory and often resolves persistent buffering that started after an update.

Tip If only Prime Video buffers while Netflix or YouTube plays smoothly, the issue is likely on Amazon's end. Check the current Prime Video server status at downdetector.com/status/amazon-prime-video before spending more time troubleshooting your home network.
