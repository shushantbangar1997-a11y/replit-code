How to Fix Common Paramount+ Problems

Updated April 2026  •  5 min read

  5. How to Fix Paramount+ Buffering & Playback Issues

Paramount+ packs CBS, MTV, Nickelodeon, BET, and live sports all in one app — but activation code failures, login blocks, and buffering are among the most-reported headaches. This guide covers the most common Paramount+ issues and gives you a clear, ordered path to fixing each one yourself.

🔑 How to Fix Paramount+ Activation Code Errors

Installing Paramount+ on a TV or streaming device shows a code you enter at paramountplus.com/activate. Here's what to do when that process fails.

  • Open paramountplus.com/activate on a phone or computer

    Make sure you are already signed in to your Paramount+ account in that browser before entering the code shown on your TV.

  • Enter the code before it expires

    Paramount+ codes expire after a few minutes. If yours has lapsed, go back to the app on your TV and generate a fresh code, then enter it right away.

  • Clear your browser cache if the activation page stalls

    In Chrome go to Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data, clear cached images and cookies, then reload paramountplus.com/activate.

  • Restart the Paramount+ app and try again

    Fully close the app on your streaming device, reopen it to generate a new code, then activate immediately at paramountplus.com/activate.

Tip If the activation page keeps spinning, try a different browser (Firefox, Safari, or Edge) or switch to a different device before requesting a new code.

👤 How to Fix Paramount+ Login Problems

Wrong password errors, a blank screen after login, or account access issues are all solvable. Work through these steps.

  • Reset your password at paramountplus.com

    Click "Sign In," then "Forgot Password?" Enter your email and follow the reset link. Check your spam folder if it doesn't arrive within a few minutes.

  • Check if you subscribed through a TV provider or Apple

    If you signed up via Xfinity, Apple, Google, or another partner, you must log in using "Sign In With TV Provider" or the corresponding partner login option.

  • Clear the app cache on your streaming device

    On Fire Stick: Settings → Applications → Manage Installed Applications → Paramount+ → Clear Cache, then Clear Data. Reopen and sign in.

  • Uninstall and reinstall the Paramount+ app

    Remove the app from your device, re-download it from your device's app store. A fresh install clears corrupted login tokens.

Tip Paramount+ allows up to 3 simultaneous streams. If you're blocked, go to your account settings at paramountplus.com, sign out of all devices, then sign back in on yours.

📺 How to Set Up Paramount+ on Roku

Paramount+ is available on all Roku devices running a current OS. Follow these steps to install and fix common Roku-specific issues.

  • Search for Paramount+ in the Roku Channel Store

    Press Home, go to "Streaming Channels," then "Search Channels." Type "Paramount Plus" and select "Add Channel."

  • Update Roku OS if the app won't install

    Settings → System → System Update → Check Now. Install any available updates, then try adding Paramount+ again.

  • Launch Paramount+ and complete activation

    Open the channel — it will display an activation code. On another device go to paramountplus.com/activate, sign in, and enter the code.

  • Fix Paramount+ crashing on Roku

    Settings → System → System Restart. If crashes continue, remove the Paramount+ channel and re-add it from the Channel Store.

Tip If live CBS or sports content is missing on Roku, sign out and sign back in to force your subscription level to refresh.

🔥 How to Set Up Paramount+ on Amazon Fire Stick

Paramount+ works on Fire Stick 4K, Fire TV Stick Lite, Fire TV Stick (2nd gen+), and Fire TV Cube. Here's how to install and troubleshoot it.

  • Find Paramount+ in the Amazon Appstore

    Press the Search icon or use Alexa and say "Paramount Plus." Select the app and choose "Get" to install it.

  • Update Fire OS if the app crashes or won't install

    Settings → My Fire TV → About → Check for System Updates. Install any pending updates and try again.

  • Sign in or complete activation

    Open Paramount+ and sign in directly, or visit paramountplus.com/activate on another device if an activation code is displayed on screen.

  • Fix app errors by clearing the cache

    Settings → Applications → Manage Installed Applications → Paramount+ → Clear Cache. If problems persist, use "Clear Data" and sign in again.

Tip If Paramount+ shows a black screen with audio, unplug the HDMI cable from the TV for 30 seconds and reconnect it, or try a different HDMI port.

📶 How to Fix Paramount+ Buffering & Playback Issues

Constant buffering, low-quality video, or streams that cut out are almost always network or device performance issues. Work through these steps in order.

  • Check your internet speed

    Paramount+ needs at least 4 Mbps for HD and 25 Mbps for 4K. Run a speed test at fast.com on the same Wi-Fi your device uses. If speed is low, restart your router and modem.

  • Use a wired connection if possible

    Wi-Fi signal weakens with distance and walls. Connecting your streaming device directly via Ethernet eliminates most wireless buffering issues.

  • Lower the video quality in Paramount+

    Open Paramount+ → Settings → Video Quality and reduce it from "Auto" or "High" to a lower setting to ease the bandwidth requirement.

  • Restart your device and clear the app cache

    Power off your streaming device for 30 seconds, then clear the Paramount+ cache before relaunching. A cold restart frees memory that causes slow playback.

Tip If only Paramount+ buffers while other apps work fine, check server status at downdetector.com/status/paramount-plus before troubleshooting your network.
